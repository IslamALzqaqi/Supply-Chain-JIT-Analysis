# 📊 Supply Chain JIT Analysis

An end-to-end Supply Chain Analytics project built using **Python, Excel, Power Query, Power Pivot, and Business Intelligence techniques** to monitor inventory performance, warehouse fulfillment, and operational efficiency through an executive dashboard and data-driven recommendations.

---

# Executive Summary

This project analyzes the operational performance of a Just-In-Time (JIT) Supply Chain using real business data.

The objective is to identify operational bottlenecks, evaluate inventory efficiency, monitor warehouse fulfillment, and provide strategic recommendations that improve service levels while reducing inventory risk.

# Business Insights

- Sporting Goods shows one of the highest warehouse fulfillment times.
- Inventory coverage is inconsistent across several product categories.
- Multiple categories suffer from overstock while others operate understock.
- Higher inventory levels do not always guarantee faster fulfillment.
- Inventory balance is the key driver of operational efficiency.

---

# Strategic Recommendations

- Reduce inventory levels for overstocked categories to improve cash flow.
- Increase safety stock for understock products with stable demand.
- Prioritize operational improvements for categories with fulfillment times above 8 days.
- Build an early warning system to detect inventory imbalance before service levels decline.
- Monitor Inventory Coverage Ratio and Warehouse Fulfillment Time together to support inventory planning decisions.
